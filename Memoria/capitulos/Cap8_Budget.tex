\chapter{Budget} \label{chap:Budget} 
This chapter presents a detailed budget for the development and deployment of the project. It has been calculated to realistically reflect the scope and complexity of the terrain generation system and the research performed to develop it. 
The cost estimation considers both human resources and computational infrastructure.

The main cost components are:

\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Working hours}: including research, implementation, experimentation, and documentation.
  \item \textbf{Computational resources}: Hardware used for the project's development and evaluation. % incluir las máquinas que usemos para 
\end{itemize}

\section{Working Hours}
The labour cost is calculated to \textbf{13.02€/hour}, based on the average salary of a junior game developer in Spain (25,000€-28,000€ annually), sourced from Glassdoor\footnote{\href{https://www.glassdoor.es/Salaries/junior-game-developer-salary-SRCH_KO0,21.htm}{Glassdoor report on game developer salary} - \url{https://tinyurl.com/glassdoor-gamedev}}.

\section{Execution Platform}
The project has been carried out on hardware personally owned by the author.
To approximate the cost of an ideal workstation, a similar, more professional computer has been assembled in the Dell website\footnote{\href{https://www.dell.com/es-es/shop/cty/pdp/spd/precision-5860-workstation/xctopt5860emea_vp?configurationid=7a5eeb22-2eb6-4b6c-9520-c7198c3efe48\#m_146} {Build in Dell} - \url{https://tinyurl.com/dell-build2}}.
It includes an Intel Xeon W5-2445 (10 cores, up to 4.6 GHz turbo frequency, 175 W) CPU and an NVIDIA RTX PRO 2000 Blackwell Workstation Edition (70 W), 16 GB GDDR7, with ECC, 4 mDP to DP GPU.
The price amounts to \textbf{5.873,74 €} with taxes.
